Stop the Damage Before it Spreads

The Four Stages of Roof Moss Growth

Identifying the stage of moss growth dictates the urgency and method of removal. [Image of the four stages of roof moss growth] If your roof is beyond Stage 2, immediate action is required to prevent structural damage.

Stage 1: Algae & Lichen

Appearance: Black streaks or thin green film. Often confused with dirt or simple aging. Impact: Primarily aesthetic, but indicates high moisture retention. The first step toward granule erosion.

Stage 2: Flat Moss

Appearance: Small, flat patches of bright green growth that adhere tightly to the shingle surface. Impact: Moss begins to wick water beneath the shingle edges, preventing proper drainage and increasing the shingle’s weight.

Stage 3: Clump Moss

Appearance: Thick, sponge-like clumps of moss up to an inch high, typically growing in northern, shaded valleys. Impact: Traps huge amounts of water, leading to shingle curling, wood rot, and failure of the roof deck beneath the colony.

Stage 4: Terminal Damage

Appearance: Widespread, deep root systems pushing entire shingle sections apart, visible lifted edges, and water stains inside the attic. Impact: The roof needs replacement. Repair is no longer viable, leading to costly structural fixes.

Soft Wash vs. Pressure Wash: A Danger Comparison

In the PNW, most asphalt shingle manufacturers void warranties if high-pressure water is used for cleaning. Our soft-wash process relies on chemistry, not force, to ensure the longevity of your roof.
