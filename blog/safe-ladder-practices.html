Safety first

Safe ladder practices for gutter cleaning

Set up your ladder correctly, protect the roofline, and avoid the emergency room. These are the steps our technicians follow on every job.

Before you climb, decide whether to DIY or hire professionals. Our gutter cleaning crews bring safety gear, insurance, and documentation that most homeowners simply don’t have.

1. Choose the right ladder

Use an extension ladder rated for at least 300 pounds. Attach ladder levelers if you’re working on sloped ground.

2. Inspect the ladder before climbing

Check rungs, locks, and feet. Replace worn rubber pads and lubricate locks to prevent sudden shifts.

3. Protect the gutter

Install a ladder stabilizer or standoff. It distributes weight and keeps you from crushing gutter lips. We recommend the Werner AC78 stabilizer for most homes.

4. Maintain three points of contact

Face the ladder, keep your belt buckle between the rails, and use a tool bucket or sling to move equipment.

5. Move frequently

Don’t overreach. Climb down, reposition, and continue. It takes longer but prevents falls and gutter damage.
