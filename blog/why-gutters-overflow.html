Overflow prevention

Why gutters overflow during Pacific Northwest storms

Overflow is rarely random. Understand the most common causes—then fix them before the next atmospheric river hits.

Seattle averages 37.5 inches of rain annually while Bellevue receives 38.7 inches according to the National Weather Service. Intense bursts overwhelm undersized or clogged systems quickly.

Watch: Overflow physics in action

Top overflow culprits

  1. Debris buildup: Needles and seed pods create dams near outlets. Regular cleaning keeps water moving.
  2. Undersized downspouts: 2x3-inch downspouts can’t handle modern rainfall intensity. Upgrade to 3x4-inch or add additional drops.
  3. Improper slope: Sagging gutters hold water. Rehang sections and add hidden hangers every 24 inches.
  4. Clogged underground drains: Tightlines and drywells fill with sediment. Jet them annually and install debris baskets.

Design fixes

We right-size gutters, install splash guards at valleys, and reroute downspouts toward dispersion trenches. Documentation keeps RainWise rebates intact and satisfies inspection teams.

Overflow FAQ

How often should gutters be cleaned in high tree zones?

Plan for quarterly cleanings when firs and cedars surround the property. Schedule extra visits after windstorms.

Do larger downspouts really matter?

Yes. Increasing cross-sectional area drastically improves flow and reduces backup during peak rainfall.

Can overflow damage foundations?

Absolutely. Chronic overflow saturates soil, leading to settlement and crawlspace flooding. Address issues quickly.
