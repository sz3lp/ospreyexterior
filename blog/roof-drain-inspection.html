Roof drain inspection checklist for Seattle & Bellevue

Commercial and multifamily roofs flood fast when drains clog. Use this step-by-step inspection to stay ahead of leaks and avoid emergency service calls.

Book roof drain audit See compliance services

Flat roof drains, scuppers, and overflow leaders need seasonal maintenance to keep stormwater moving into Seattle and Bellevue utility systems. Document your inspections carefully—RainWise rebates and capital improvement plans depend on accurate records.

Tools and prep

  • GFI-protected extension cord and wet/dry vacuum
  • Hand scoop, stiff brush, and hose for clearing bowls
  • Inspection camera or smartphone for documentation
  • Safety gear: harness, roof anchor, cones, and barricade tape

Roof drain anatomy

Inspection steps

  1. Secure the site: Set perimeter cones and tie-off points before stepping onto the roof.
  2. Remove debris: Lift the strainer, scoop organics, and vacuum standing water from the bowl.
  3. Check connections: Inspect clamp rings, gaskets, and bolt tension. Replace cracked components immediately.
  4. Flush the leader: Run water through the drain, confirm flow at the downspout or interior conductor head, and note any slow discharge.
  5. Document findings: Photograph every drain, log flow results, and note recommended repairs or compliance actions.

When to call a professional

If you find ponding, deteriorated membrane around the drain bowl, or slow internal conductors, schedule a professional audit. Our team coordinates with property managers, creates stamped reports, and escalates repairs before tenants experience leaks.
