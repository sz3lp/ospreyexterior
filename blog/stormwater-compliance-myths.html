Stormwater compliance myths Eastside property managers still believe

City codes evolve quickly. Here’s what’s fact—and what’s fiction—about stormwater maintenance obligations in Bellevue, Seattle, and the greater Eastside.

Skipping inspections or paperwork can jeopardize RainWise rebates and expose owners to fines. We partner with municipal reviewers to keep conveyance systems flowing and documentation airtight.

Myth 1: Annual inspections are optional

Reality: Bellevue and Seattle require annual inspections for private drainage systems. Bellevue’s Utilities Code Section 24.06.065 mandates reports for flow control structures, while Seattle Stormwater Manual Volume 3 Section 4.1 outlines maintenance frequencies.

Myth 2: RainWise rebates cover all maintenance

Reality: Rebates offset installation costs, but you must budget for ongoing cleaning, inspections, and documentation. Our compliance specialists log each visit so your rebate remains active.

Myth 3: Photos aren’t necessary

Reality: Inspectors expect visual proof. We upload high-resolution images of gutters, cisterns, overflows, and discharge points to shared folders so you can respond quickly during audits.

Myth 4: Overflow only matters during storms

Reality: Standing water accelerates roof degradation and invites citations. Routine cleaning and drain balancing keep systems ready year-round, not just during atmospheric rivers.

Stormwater compliance FAQ

How often should we clean gutters tied to stormwater systems?

Plan for quarterly cleanings in heavily treed areas and biannual service elsewhere. Keep logs alongside inspection reports.

Can maintenance crews submit compliance paperwork?

They can, but most facilities outsource to specialists to avoid missed signatures or incomplete photos. We submit documents through municipal portals on your behalf.

What happens if we miss an inspection?

Expect a notice of violation and a short window to provide evidence. Schedule catch-up service immediately and document corrective actions.
