Process

Four-step gutter cleaning process built for the Eastside

Every visit is standardized so you receive consistent quality and documentation regardless of crew.

1

Site protection & safety check

We set up ladder guards, tie-offs, and ground tarps to protect landscaping and siding before stepping on the roof.

2

Roofline debris removal

Hand scoop, backpack vacuum, and air blowers clear roof valleys, gutters, and leaf traps without spreading mess across your yard.

3

Downspout flush & flow test

Each downspout is pressure flushed, then checked at grade to confirm water is moving to drains, cisterns, or dispersal trenches.

4

Photo report & maintenance plan

Receive a same-day email with before/after photos, noted repairs, and the recommended service cadence for your tree canopy. We also link follow-up resources like our Eastside stormwater compliance guide so you can stay ahead of inspections.

FAQ

FAQ

Eastside gutter cleaning questions answered

Weather trends and maintenance rhythms we monitor for homes from Bellevue to Sammamish.

How often should my gutters be cleaned?

Phantom Lake homes backing wetlands should clean gutters every season to prevent overflow from saturating crawl spaces.

Do you clean up the debris?

Yes. We bag and remove all gutter debris from site unless you prefer it placed in yard waste. Driveways, decks, and patios are rinsed before we leave.

Can you service gutter guards?

We maintain perforated and micro-mesh guards, removing panels where necessary to extract composted debris and reinstalling them for proper slope.

What about steep or tall roofs?

Our rope access team handles three-story walkouts and 12/12 pitches. We document anchor points and follow L&I fall protection standards every visit.

Do you also clean exterior windows?

Yes. Add window cleaning to any gutter visit and we will squeegee exterior glass up to three stories, wipe frames, and ensure screens are rinsed before we leave.

How soon can you schedule?

Prime booking windows are 24-48 hours. During heavy rain alerts we open early and late appointments to stop overflow before it damages fascia or basements.
