What’s Happening on Site

  • Insufficient net free vent area for attic square footage.
  • Ridge-only exhaust without enough intake at soffit level.
  • Blocked baffles reducing airflow from eaves into attic channels.
  • Mixed vent types creating short-circuit airflow instead of full attic sweep.

Ventilation imbalance is especially common in Redmond Ridge homes with complex roof geometry and partial attic compartments.

Why This Matters for Escrow, Lending, and Insurance

Ventilation compliance notes are often tied to moisture and roof longevity concerns. In escrow, these findings can become mandatory correction items because they influence both future maintenance risk and present-condition confidence.

Buyers and lenders respond better when the correction plan explains airflow logic in plain language rather than generic statements. Specific intake/exhaust changes reduce objection cycles.
