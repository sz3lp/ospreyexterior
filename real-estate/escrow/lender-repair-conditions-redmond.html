What Lender Conditions Usually Require

  • Proof that flagged defects were corrected by a licensed contractor.
  • Before/after or completion photos with dates and location context.
  • Scope language that matches the condition letter wording.
  • Completion timeline that fits within contingency and rate-lock windows.

Underwriters rarely accept vague statements. They want evidence: what was found, what was corrected, when it was completed, and who performed the work. Our documentation is built for that exact review path.

Why Lender Conditions Block Funding

Lender conditions appear when an appraisal, inspection, or underwriting review flags exterior defects that could affect property value or insurability. Until those conditions are cleared with acceptable documentation, funding does not release.

Rate locks expire. Closing dates slip. The faster you deliver underwriter-ready proof, the less risk of last-minute extension requests or renegotiation. We structure our scopes and closeout packages so agent teams can forward one file and move to clear-to-close.
