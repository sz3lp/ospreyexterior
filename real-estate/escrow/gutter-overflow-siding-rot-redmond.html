What's Happening on Site

  • Downspout outlets undersized for roof area, forcing backflow at storms.
  • Gutter pitch drift causing standing water and spillover at corners.
  • Clogged valley discharge points saturating siding at concentrated runoff lines.
  • Repeated wetting leading to soft trim, paint failure, and localized rot.

Overflow-to-rot patterns are especially common in Education Hill homes with mature tree canopy and high fall leaf load.

Why This Matters for Escrow, Lending, and Insurance

Overflow damage reads as an active envelope problem, not a cosmetic defect. Buyers worry about hidden sheathing deterioration, and lenders can require documented repairs before release of final funds.

If the moisture source is not corrected first, patch-only repairs fail and become negotiation points. A clear drainage-first scope keeps escrow discussions focused and credible.

Inspection Language We Usually Resolve

Reports often include "overflow evidence," "moisture-damaged siding," and "recommend licensed contractor evaluation." We pinpoint whether failure comes from slope, outlet sizing, blockage, or attachment movement. Then we tie siding repair boundaries to corrected flow paths so buyers can see why the fix is durable rather than temporary.

Escrow Outcome Focus

This problem becomes expensive when cause and effect are treated separately. We keep them together: drainage correction first, then exterior material repair. That sequencing protects workmanship and gives lenders a clear narrative that active moisture exposure has been resolved before finish restoration.
